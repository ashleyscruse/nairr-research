## Morning Block 1 — Daily Review + Finish Writing (9:00 – 10:15)

- **Purpose:** Get every paper to "complete first draft" status before peer review
- **Format:** Stand-up + independent writing
- **Internal schedule (from facilitator guide):**
  - 9:00 – 9:15: Daily review, where is everyone, who has a complete draft
  - 9:15 – 10:15: Finish writing the remaining sections: Discussion, Conclusion, Abstract
- **Writing order reminder** (from [publication-stage.md:131-141](publication-stage.md)):
  - Discussion is interpretation + limitations from Day 4, structured for the venue
  - Conclusion is 3 to 4 sentences restating the contribution
  - Abstract is written last because you cannot summarize what you have not written
- **Abstract structure to teach:**
  - Question / problem (1 sentence)
  - What you did (2 to 3 sentences)
  - What you found (2 to 3 sentences with specific numbers)
  - Why it matters / contribution (1 sentence)
  - Target length: 150 to 250 words
- **AI prompt pattern for the abstract** (from [publication-stage.md:154](publication-stage.md)):
  - "Here is my Results section draft: [paste]. Review for: are all claims supported by specific numbers, is the writing concise, are there unsupported assertions"

## Morning Block 2 — Structured Peer Review (10:15 – 11:15)

- **Purpose:** Every paper gets a fresh pair of eyes on it before submission
- **Format:** Paired exchange using a structured template
- **Lead resources:**
  - [docs/05-Publication/02-peer-review.md](docs/05-Publication/02-peer-review.md)
  - [templates/peer-review.md](templates/peer-review.md)
- **Internal schedule (from facilitator guide):**
  - 10:15 – 11:15: Peer review, exchange drafts, fill out review template, give feedback
- **The six review questions** (from [publication-stage.md:240-249](publication-stage.md)):
  - Is the research question clear (can you state it in one sentence after reading the intro)
  - Is the gap convincing (after reading Related Work, do you believe this has not been done)
  - Is the methodology reproducible (could you run this study from the Methods section alone)
  - Are the results supported (does every claim have a number or figure behind it)
  - Are the limitations honest (did the author miss any obvious ones)
  - Is it readable (any sections that are confusing, too long, or too vague)
- **Pairing strategy:**
  - Pair across discipline when possible (a non-domain reviewer catches what the domain expert assumes)
  - Pair across writing environment if helpful (LaTeX person reviewing a Word person to check formatting)
  - Avoid pairing co-authors or close collaborators

## Afternoon Block 1 — Submission Logistics (1:00 – 1:30)

- **Purpose:** Every participant leaves knowing exactly how to submit, by when, and at what cost
- **Format:** Lecture + decision-making
- **Lead resources:**
  - [docs/05-Publication/03-submission.md](docs/05-Publication/03-submission.md)
  - [templates/submission-plan.md](templates/submission-plan.md)
- **Content to cover** (from [publication-stage.md:204-234](publication-stage.md)):
  - What to include (PDF or Word doc, supplementary materials, code link, data availability statement)
  - Anonymization for double-blind review (remove name, affiliation, self-citations, file metadata)
  - Submission costs (submission fees $0 to $100, registration $200 to $800, page charges, open access fees $500 to $3000, color figure charges)
  - Asking your institution about publication support funds before paying out of pocket
- **Submission plan template** to complete:
  - Target venue + deadline
  - Format requirements confirmed
  - Anonymization done (if required)
  - Co-author approvals (if applicable)
  - Submission fee paid (if applicable)
