# Target Venues

Your target venue determines the format, length, audience, review criteria, and deadline for your paper. Pick it now, not after you write.

## Conference vs. Journal

| Factor | Conference | Journal |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Timeline | Fixed deadlines, faster turnaround | Rolling or quarterly, slower review |
| Length | 6-10 pages typical | 15-30 pages typical |
| Review | Usually single round | Multiple revision rounds |
| Networking | Presentation opportunity | No in-person component |
| Best for | Preliminary results, new ideas | Complete, polished studies |

For a week-long project, a **conference paper** or **workshop paper** is the most realistic target. You can expand to a journal paper later.

## How to Find the Right Venue

1. Look at where your key papers were published
2. Check the venue's scope (does your topic fit?)
3. Check the acceptance rate (is it realistic?)
4. Check the timeline (when is the next deadline?)

## Submission Costs

| Fee Type | When | Typical Range |
|----------|------|---------------|
| Submission fee | When you submit | $0-100 |
| Registration fee | When accepted (conferences) | $200-800 |
| Page charges | If over page limit | $100-200/page |
| Open access fee | If you want open access (journals) | $500-3000 |

**Ask your institution** about publication support funds before paying out of pocket.

## Activity

Identify 2-3 target venues. For each, record the name, type, next deadline, page limit, and why your work fits. Add this to your research brief.
