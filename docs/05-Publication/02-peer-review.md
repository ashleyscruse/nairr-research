# Peer Review

You are too close to your own paper. A fresh pair of eyes catches things you miss.

## How It Works

Pair with another participant. Exchange drafts. Each reviewer reads the paper and answers six questions using the peer review template.

## The Six Questions

### 1. Is the research question clear?
Can you state it in one sentence after reading the introduction?

### 2. Is the gap convincing?
After reading Related Work, do you believe this has not been done?

### 3. Is the methodology reproducible?
Could you run this study from the Methods section alone?

### 4. Are the results supported?
Does every claim have a number or figure behind it?

### 5. Are the limitations honest?
Did the author miss any obvious ones?

### 6. Is it readable?
Any sections that are confusing, too long, or too vague?

## After Receiving Feedback

1. Read all comments before changing anything
2. Address the biggest issue first
3. Do not argue with feedback, fix or explain
4. If a reviewer was confused, the writing is the problem, not the reviewer

## Overall Rating

- **Ready to submit with minor edits** -- fix typos and formatting, then submit
- **Needs one more revision pass** -- address feedback, have someone read again
- **Needs significant rework** -- revisit the core argument or results before submitting
