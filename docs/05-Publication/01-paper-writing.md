# Writing Your Paper

## Three Writing Environments

Pick whichever you are fastest in.

| Your Situation | Use |
|---|---|
| Targeting ACM, IEEE, Springer | LaTeX (VS Code or Overleaf) |
| Targeting education, health, social science journals | Word or Google Docs |
| You know LaTeX | VS Code with LaTeX Workshop |
| You want LaTeX without installing anything | Overleaf |
| You want real-time collaboration | Overleaf or Google Docs |
| You just want words on paper | Whatever you are fastest in |

## Paper Structure

| Section | Source From This Week |
|---------|----------------------|
| Abstract (150-250 words) | Written last |
| Introduction (1-2 pages) | Research Brief |
| Related Work (1-2 pages) | Literature review from Stage 1 |
| Methodology (1-2 pages) | Methodology Document from Stage 2 |
| Results (1-2 pages) | Analysis Document from Stage 4 |
| Discussion (1 page) | Interpretation and limitations from Stage 4 |
| Conclusion (0.5 page) | Summary of everything |
| References | Zotero collection |

## Writing Order

1. **Methodology** (already written in Stage 2)
2. **Results** (already written in Stage 4)
3. **Related Work** (from Stage 1 lit review)
4. **Introduction** (now you know what you found)
5. **Discussion** (interpretation + limitations)
6. **Conclusion** (3-4 sentences)
7. **Abstract** (write last)

Most sections already exist in some form. Writing the paper is assembly and polishing.

## Using AI for Drafting

> "Here is my methodology document: [paste]. Draft a Methodology section for a conference paper targeting [venue]. Use past tense. Target 1.5 pages."

> "Review my Results section: [paste]. Are all claims supported by numbers? Is the writing concise? Suggest revisions."

### What AI Should NOT Do
- Write your research question
- Interpret your results
- Generate fake data
- Add citations it cannot verify
