# Stage 6: Writing Your Paper

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

- Structure a research paper using standard sections
- Use AI to accelerate drafting without losing your voice
- Write each section efficiently by drawing on work from previous stages

## Paper Structure

Most computational research papers follow this structure:

| Section | Length | What It Contains | Source From This Week |
|---------|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| Abstract | 150-250 words | Summary of question, method, results, significance | Written last |
| Introduction | 1-2 pages | Problem, motivation, contribution | Your research brief (Stage 2) |
| Related Work | 1-2 pages | What has been done, what the gap is | Your literature review (Stage 2) |
| Methodology | 1-2 pages | Data, pipeline, models, evaluation approach | Your methodology doc (Stage 3) |
| Results | 1-2 pages | What you found, with figures and tables | Your analysis (Stage 5) |
| Discussion | 1 page | What it means, limitations, future work | Your interpretation (Stage 5) |
| Conclusion | 0.5 page | Restate contribution and main finding | Summary of everything |
| References | Varies | All cited works | Your Zotero collection |

## Writing Order

Do not write from top to bottom. Write in this order:

1. **Methodology** (you already have this from Stage 3)
2. **Results** (your figures and analysis from Stage 5)
3. **Related Work** (your literature review from Stage 2)
4. **Introduction** (now that you know what you found, frame the problem)
5. **Discussion** (interpretation and limitations)
6. **Conclusion** (restate the contribution)
7. **Abstract** (summarize the whole paper in 200 words)

Each section has already been drafted in some form during the week. Writing the paper is assembly and polishing, not starting from scratch.

## Using AI for Paper Writing

AI accelerates the drafting process. You still own every word.

### Drafting a section

In Claude (web or Code):

> "Here is my methodology document: [paste]. Here is the data I used: [paste table of datasets]. Draft a Methodology section for a conference paper. Use past tense. Be specific about data sources, preprocessing steps, model configurations, and evaluation metrics. Target length: 1.5 pages."

### Improving a draft

> "Here is my Results section draft: [paste]. Review it for: (1) Are all claims supported by specific numbers from my results? (2) Is the writing concise? (3) Are there any unsupported assertions? Suggest revisions."

### What AI should NOT do

- Write your research question for you (that is your intellectual contribution)
- Interpret your results for you (only you know what the results mean in context)
- Generate fake data or results
- Add citations it cannot verify

## Writing Tips

**Be specific.** Not "the model performed well" but "XGBoost achieved an R-squared of 0.78 on the held-out test set, compared to 0.72 for Random Forest."

**Use active voice.** Not "the data was processed" but "we processed the data."

**One idea per paragraph.** If a paragraph covers two ideas, split it.

**Every figure needs a reference.** If you include Figure 3, the text must say "As shown in Figure 3, ..."

**Every claim needs support.** Either cite a paper or point to your own results.

## Activity

1. Assemble your existing materials (methodology doc, research brief, analysis write-up, figures)
2. Draft the Methodology and Results sections (these should be fastest since you already have the content)
3. Draft the Related Work section from your literature review
4. Draft the Introduction
5. Peer review with another participant

## Checkpoint

You move to the next session when you have a complete first draft of all sections.
